Plus 3/4 years? *** Sherlocke Holmes, his real legal name, opened a private investigations agency a few years ago, and quietly slide into local society. He immediately started appearing at entertainment and social events attired as the fictional detective, with historical English gentleman manners to match. He soon made a name for himself as a solver of difficult problems. He did not publicize his successes. The recipients of his skills often did that for him. As his reputation grew, and his contacts widened, he started arranging work to handle situations were simply determining what had really occurred, or who was responsible, was not enough. A follower of Owl, he seldom tells all he knows, but does not deliberately hold back information important to completing contracted work. Those that know him, say he is trustworthy, but some find him a bit creepy, quite apart from his full lifestyle emulation of the fictional detective. His public office has the feel of a historical english sitting or drawing room. It is designed to make visitors feel like guests not clients. It has all of the modern office technology available, but that is downplayed. There are various fictional detective memorabilia included in the decor. Mostly Sherlocke Holmes related, but other fictional detectives are featured as well. None of this is explicitly framed or spotlighted. It is all part of the ambiance, so the place feels lived in, not like a museum. The office is not the only place to meet him. He travels the gamut of what society provides, from the lowest dive bar to near the top of society circles.
